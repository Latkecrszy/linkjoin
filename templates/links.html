Tutorial

Welcome to LinkJoin! This tutorial will guide you through how to use the site and all of its features. In each step, you'll find a description of a part of the page, along with a demo to play around with. Without further ado, let's get started!

Take Notes

You can take notes on your meetings right in LinkJoin! Click on this box to view your meeting notes, if you have any.

Change Settings

Customize your LinkJoin experience and functionality with the settings menu. In settings, you can hide and show this tutorial, change how your links are sorted, and adjust many other settings.

Settings

Make a Link

To schedule a link, all you need to do is click the plus button and fill in the info.

Disable and Enable Links

Near the right side of your link, you'll see a blue switch. You can use this to change whether or not your link will open automatically (blue means it will, gray means it will not).

Dot Menu

On the far right side of your link, you'll see 3 dots. That is the dot menu, where various actions for your link are stored.

Delete Links

The second button of the dot menu is the delete button. This allows you to delete links from your LinkJoin. After links are deleted, they are moved into the deleted links section, which can be access through settings.

Share Links

Below the delete button of the dot menu, you'll find the share button. This lets you copy a link that you can send to others that will add your link with all its information to their LinkJoin. This feature is especially useful for sending a link out to large volumes of people, because it guarantees that they have the correct time and date with just the click of a link.

Take Notes

The penultimate button in the dot menu is the notes button. This button creates a note for your meeting, letting you take notes about it right in LinkJoin. These notes are auto-saved within LinkJoin, and can be accessed by clicking the notes button again, or by clicking the Meeting Notes button and searching for the meeting.

Copy Link

The last button you will always see in the dot menu of your links is the copy link button. All this button does is copy your meeting link. This is not the same as the Share button, which copies a link that will add your scheduled meeting to someone else's LinkJoin.
